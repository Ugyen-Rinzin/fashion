HISTORY

HISTORY OF FASHION

Over the ages, fashion has been the art of expressing oneself and one's community via apparel and accessories. It has seen huge change. Clothing was a common way for ancient societies like Egypt, Greece, and Rome to express their social standing and cultural identity. In Europe during the Middle Ages, the church and aristocracy had a big influence on fashion, with ornate clothing serving as a symbol of authority and riches. The emergence of fashion centers such as Paris throughout the 18th century led to the concept of haute couture. Through mass manufacture, the 19th century Industrial Revolution made fashionable apparel more affordable, democratizing fashion. The flapper movement brought about dramatic developments in the 20th century.
